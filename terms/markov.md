Markov assumption: The idea that the future (next state) depends only on the present (current state), not the past.

Markov property: The formal statement/mathematical definition of Markov assumption.

Markov chain: A sequence of states/stochastic process that obeys the Markov property.

Markov model: Any model that uses the Markov property. (A Markov chain is the simplest kind of Markov model)

Hidden Markov model: The true states are hidden, we only observe outputs related to those states. (Hidden Markov model = Markov chain + hidden states + observation layer)

Markov decision process: A Markov model where an agent takes actions and actions influence transitions and rewards. (MDP = Markov chain + actions + rewards)
